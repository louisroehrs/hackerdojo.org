Hacker Dojo Policies
All policies in one place. Subject to revision through voting. Last revised April 19, 2024.
Core Principle: Be excellent to each other.
Contents

Facilities
Operating Hours
10 AM – 9 PM, seven days a week. Members have 24/7 access via keycard.
Sign-In
All guests must register upon arrival. Refusal to sign in or accept policies prohibits entry.
Communal Space
No personal storage permitted. All equipment is shared first-come-first-serve. Donated or loaned items may be damaged or removed without liability.
Security
Members must keep doors secured outside business hours. No propping doors open or tailgating.
Sleeping
Prohibited except during approved events.
Cleanliness
Users are responsible for cleanup. Cleaning supplies are available in the kitchen.
Pets
Allowed if potty-trained and friendly. Disruptive animals must be removed.
Attire
Clothes and shoes are mandatory at all times.
Liability for Damage
Users are financially responsible for breaking facility components.
Donations
Require director approval before bringing items. LCD monitors are always welcome.
Scientific Work
No fume hood requirements. No pathogen research. No food storage contamination.
Refrigerator
Member-only use. Food removed daily by 10 PM. 24-hour maximum storage.
Parking
Maximum 36-hour vehicle limit. No sleeping in vehicles. Lot is not for storage or camping.
Network Equipment
No personal routers or hotspots without staff approval. See Network Policies for technical details.
Membership
Access
Immediate upon membership. Non-transferable.
Accounts
Individual-only. Login sharing is prohibited.
Refunds
No refunds or prorated credits. Members are responsible for pausing before renewal.
Visitors & Drop-Ins
Guest Definition
Non-members or event attendees. A $20/day donation is encouraged.
Guest Limitations
No free event access, no member WiFi, no voting rights, no accounts.
Children
Permitted with full parental supervision and responsibility.
Extended Stays
Visiting 2+ days requires membership.
Networks

See the full Network Policies page for technical specifications. Summary: guests cannot set up access points. Members may for testing only, with strict channel and power limits. Violations can result in loss of AP privileges or membership termination.

Harassment
Hacker Dojo maintains a firm commitment to safety and inclusivity. A separate Harassment Policy governs unacceptable behavior. All members and visitors are expected to treat each other with respect.
Event Policies
No NDAs
Organizers cannot require NDAs for event attendance.
Advance Notice
Minimum 48-hour advance notice required. Board review maximum 5 weeks in advance.
Timing
Member weekday events must start at 5:30 PM or later. Daytime events require committee approval — contact events@hackerdojo.com.
Organizer Orientation
Event organizers must complete an orientation quiz every 6 months. Private events require committee approval.
Branding
Using "Hacker Dojo" in an event title requires board approval.
Advertising
No external advertising until the event is approved.
Charging Attendees
Event hosts may charge for events. Suggested 20% member donation from net revenue.
Member Attendance
Members attend all member-run events free (excludes tangible resources like food or supplies). Private events require being listed as an attendee.
Event Execution
Setup
Maximum 1 hour setup time. Overlaps with previous event teardown. Organizer locates and arranges all equipment and furniture.
Sign-In
All attendees must sign in. Organizers should encourage donations and membership sign-ups.
Organizer Presence
Organizer must remain present for the full duration of the event.
Teardown & Cleanup
Maximum 1 hour teardown. Room returned to posted standard configuration. Organizer responsible for all trash removal and kitchen supply replenishment for large events.
Damage
Organizer is liable for facility damage during their event.
Policy Process
Do-ocracy
Members initiate changes by leading implementation. If you see something that should change, step up and do it.
Reimbursements
No reimbursements without prior director approval.
Feedback
Members are welcome to suggest policy modifications. Board of Directors retains discretionary authority.

Submitted by Tanya Troshyna, Board Secretary. Updated by Emily Johnson, Board Chair. Last revised April 19, 2024.
