Bylaws
Governing document of Hacker Dojo, a California Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation. Adopted December 21, 2021. Certified December 21, 2022.

§1 — Corporate Name

The organization is named Hacker Dojo.

§2 — Corporate Office

Principal office: 855 Maude Avenue, Mountain View, CA 94043. The board may relocate the principal office and establish branch offices as needed.

§3 — Corporate Purpose
"Promote and encourage technical, scientific and artistic skills and knowledge by providing community centers for hackers and thinkers to meet, discuss, teach, learn and create."
§5 — Irrevocable Dedication of Assets
  • Assets dedicated exclusively to public and charitable purposes
  • No net earnings inure to private individuals, directors, or officers
  • Upon dissolution, assets distribute to IRC §501(c)(3) organizations
§6 — No Voting Members

The corporation has no voting members. The board may create nonvoting member classes with discretionary rights.

§7 — Board of Directors
Size & Terms
  • Size: 3–7 directors (initially 5)
  • Classes: Three staggered classes for continuity
  • Terms: 3 years per director
  • Compensation: None, though approved expenses are reimbursed
  • No term limits
Powers

The board manages all corporation activities and may appoint/remove officers, relocate the principal office, borrow money, and determine charitable funding recipients.

Meetings
  • Annual meeting: Third Wednesday of February (or board-designated date)
  • Special meetings: Called by chair, president, vice president, secretary, or any two directors
  • Remote participation: Permitted if all members can communicate and participate concurrently
  • Quorum: Majority of current directors
Removal

The board may remove any director with or without cause. Automatic removal occurs after 3 consecutive missed meetings unless an approved leave of absence, illness/disability waiver, or board reinstatement vote is in place.

Committees
Audit Committee
Minimum 2 directors. No employees, officers, or compensated consultants. Recommends auditors, reviews and accepts/rejects audits.
Compensation Committee
Minimum 3 directors, no non-directors. Reviews CEO, CFO, and officer compensation annually.
Executive Committee
2+ directors. Has full board authority between meetings. Actions reported to full board for ratification at next meeting.
Investment Committee
Minimum 3 directors. Prudent investor standard. May retain professional managers and develop annual investment policy.
§8 — Officers

Required officers: President, Secretary, Chief Financial Officer. Additional officers (Vice Presidents, etc.) are optional.

  • Secretary and CFO cannot simultaneously serve as president or board chair
  • One person may hold multiple offices
  • Officer compensation is fixed by the board and must be reasonable
Key Responsibilities
President
CEO and general manager. Supervises all activities, affairs, and officers. Presides at board meetings in chair's absence.
Secretary
Maintains meeting minutes, articles, bylaws, and membership records. Provides all required meeting notices. Maintains corporate seal.
Chief Financial Officer
Maintains accounting records, provides financial reports, manages deposits and disbursements per board order. May be required to be bonded.
§9 — Contracts with Officers & Directors

Officers and directors cannot have undisclosed material financial interests in corporation contracts. Required approval process: full disclosure, majority board authorization (excluding interested directors), determination that corporation cannot obtain better terms, and confirmation the transaction is fair and beneficial.

§11 — Indemnification

The corporation indemnifies directors, officers, and employees against expenses, judgments, fines, and settlements in proceedings to the fullest legal extent. Expenses may be advanced before proceedings conclude upon an undertaking to repay.

§13 — Corporate Records

The corporation maintains accounting records, minutes of all meetings, and tax returns. Contracts are executed by the CEO or CFO and secretary, or by board-delegated officers.

§16 — Fiscal Year & Annual Report
  • Fiscal year: Calendar year (January 1 – December 31)
  • Annual report: Sent within 120 days of fiscal year-end, containing assets/liabilities, revenue, expenses, and an independent accountant report or officer certificate
§19 — Amendment

Bylaws may be altered, amended, restated, or repealed via majority vote of current board directors. New bylaws may be adopted by the same process.

Certified by David E. Weekly, Secretary. Adopted December 21, 2021. No amendments since adoption. Certified December 21, 2022.
