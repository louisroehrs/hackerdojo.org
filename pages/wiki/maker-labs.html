Maker Labs
Tools, equipment, and safety rules for the Hacker Dojo makerspace.
Safety first. Do not enter the makerspace intoxicated. Do not use tools when tired. Follow all safety guidelines below.
Housekeeping Rules
  • Do not enter the makerspace intoxicated
  • Do not use tools when you are tired
  • Food and drinks are not allowed in the makerspace
  • Do not lean on the tools or machines
  • Wear appropriate attire: closed-toe shoes, protective clothing, eyewear, masks, or earplugs as needed for the equipment in use
  • Gloves are permitted except when using power tools (gloves can catch and pull hands into rotating equipment)

Fire safety equipment is available on-site. If something catches fire, use the extinguisher. Know where it is before you start working.

Equipment
Laser Cutter

The makerspace features a 100-watt CO2 laser cutter. Training is required before use. Check with staff for scheduling a training session. The laser cutter includes a maintenance subsection — see posted documentation near the machine for maintenance procedures.

Machine Tools

A variety of machine tools are available for member use after completing the required safety training. Specific tools and availability are posted in the makerspace. Contact staff or check the wiki for current equipment listings.

3D Printers

Multiple 3D printers are available. Training is recommended for first-time users. Staff and experienced members are available to help with setup, slicing, and print troubleshooting.

Electronics Lab

Separate from the makerspace, the electronics lab features:

  • Oscilloscopes
  • Bench power supplies
  • Soldering stations
  • Hand tools and component stock
Training & Onboarding

Most makerspace equipment requires completing a brief training before independent use. This protects both you and the equipment. Ask a staff member or reach out through Discord to arrange training.

Have a tool, machine, or project to contribute? Contact the makerspace team or post in Discord. Donations are welcome with director approval.
